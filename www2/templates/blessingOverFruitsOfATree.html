Fruits get the "Ha-etz" blessing. In Jewish law, a fruit is defined as something growing from a perennial tree that does not renew its stem and does not grow too close to the ground. Thus, apples, grapes, nuts (except peanuts) and figs are fruit, but strawberries, watermelon and bananas are not.

After eating most tree-fruit, say Borei Nefashot. However, after eating grapes, figs, dates, pomegranates, and olives, say Al Hapeirot.

Blessing Over Fruits of a Tree:

Blessed are You, L-rd our G‑d, King of the universe, who creates the fruit of the tree.
Baruch atah A-donay, Elo-heinu Melech Ha’Olam borei pri ha-aitz.
